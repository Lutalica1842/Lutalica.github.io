Interaction Design and Game Mechanics

Interaction Design

In game design, good interaction design and game mechanics can improve players' participation and immersion, making them more willing to invest in the game. For example, an intuitive and easy-to-use user interface can reduce players' learning costs and operation difficulty and improve the accessibility and ease of use of the game. At the same time, well-designed game mechanics can create rich and diverse gameplay and challenges, increase the fun and playability of the game, and generate continued interest and motivation for players.

Game Mechanics

In the literature, many studies focus on the impact of interaction design and game mechanics on user experience. For example, Rogers and Chaparro (2018) pointed out that good interaction design can improve users’ satisfaction and quality of experience and increase users’ willingness to use and loyalty. In addition, Björk and Holopainen (2005) conducted an in-depth study on game mechanics, proposed several basic mechanism types in game design, and discussed the impact of different mechanisms on game experience. These studies provide important theoretical support for our in-depth understanding of the role of interaction design and game mechanics in game design.
